The Librarian's Shelf

Website Site Plan

Site Name

The Librarian's Shelf

This name evokes the image of a trusted librarian's personal bookshelf — curated, intentional, and full of hidden gems. The word "shelf" implies a physical, tactile relationship with books, reinforcing the cozy and intimate tone of the site. It also scales naturally as a brand: it is short, memorable, and genre-neutral enough to house spiritual, historical, and science titles under one roof.

Optional domain: the-librarians-shelf.com

Site Purpose

The Librarian's Shelf is a personal book recommendation blog written from the perspective of a single reader — a librarian — who reads deeply across three genres: spiritual wisdom, historical non-fiction, and science. The site provides:

  • Thoughtful, long-form reviews and personal reflections for each featured book
  • A browsable catalog of all reviewed books, filterable by genre
  • Atmospheric, original artwork paired with each review to visually capture the mood of the book
  • A personal reading list feature that lets visitors save books to their browser using localStorage
  • A sense of warmth and literary community for readers who want more than a star rating

The site is not a store, a database, or a social platform. It is a reading journal with a voice — designed to inspire curiosity and guide readers toward their next meaningful book.

Scenarios

The following questions represent what a typical visitor — a curious, thoughtful reader — might come to the site looking to answer:

  1. "I want to get into spiritual reading but I don't know where to start. What would the librarian recommend for a complete beginner?"
  2. "I just finished a book about ancient Rome and I loved it. Does the librarian have any other historical recommendations with that same epic, immersive feeling?"
  3. "Can I save a list of books I want to read later without having to make an account?"
  4. "What is the librarian currently reading, and when will the next review be posted?"

Color Scheme

The palette is inspired by a candlelit study — deep greens of old library walls, the amber of a warm lamp, and the cream of aged pages.

Deep Forest Green

#2C4A3E

Used for the page background, navigation bar, and section dividers. This is the dominant color that sets the cozy, enveloping tone of the site.

Parchment Cream

#F5ECD7

Used for card backgrounds, body text on dark surfaces, and content section backgrounds. Mimics the feel of aged paper and keeps reading comfortable.

Amber Gold

#C8922A

Used for headings, links, hover states, accent borders, and the site name. Provides warmth and draws the eye to important elements.

Dark Walnut Brown

#2A1F14

Used for body text on light (cream) backgrounds. Rich and warm — easier on the eyes than pure black and more in keeping with the aesthetic.

Muted Sage

#5C7A6E

Used for subtle borders, card outlines, secondary UI elements, and hover backgrounds. Acts as a bridge between the deep green and cream tones.

Typography

Three typefaces work together to create a literary, handcrafted feeling without sacrificing readability.

The Librarian's Shelf

Dancing Script — Script Accent

Used for the site name in the header, pull quotes inside article pages, and decorative section labels. Adds a personal, handwritten warmth that feels like a librarian's handwriting on a card catalog.

A History of the Ancient World

Playfair Display — Display Headings

Used for page titles, book titles, section headings (h1–h3), and card headers. Its high contrast between thick and thin strokes gives it a classic editorial quality suited to literary content.

There is something sacred about the act of reading — a quiet conversation between a mind long gone and one very much alive. This is why we read.

Lora — Body Text

Used for all paragraph text, list items, captions, and descriptions. Lora is a well-kerned serif designed specifically for comfortable long-form reading on screens — perfect for book reviews.
